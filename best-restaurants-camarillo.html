Best Restaurants in Camarillo (2026)

From a legendary meat market to a Peruvian gem to the most unique bar in Ventura County — the best places to eat in Camarillo, hand-picked by The Camarillo Caller.

By Gabrielle Ridgeway Updated March 2026 🍽 5 picks 📍 Camarillo, CA
These are my personal picks based on living in Camarillo and knowing the food scene. I only recommend places I'd send a friend. None of these listings are paid or sponsored.

Camarillo doesn't always get credit for its food scene — but it should. Between the authentic taquerias, the chef-driven hidden gems, and the spots that just feel like home, there's a lot more going on here than people realize. These are my five favorites. The places I actually go back to.

#1 Pick
Mexican · Taqueria

Establos Meat Market

💰 $ ⚡ Quick service 🥡 Great for takeout 👥 Casual / groups

If you only go to one place on this list, make it Establos. It's the kind of spot that looks unassuming from the outside but completely delivers every single time — big, filling portions, super flavorful meats, and that authentic market-plus-taqueria feel where everything tastes freshly made. The lengua and cabeza are standouts, and if you haven't tried their ceviche or chicharrón tacos, you're missing out.

My go-to order is the tamales and a breakfast burrito, but honestly it's hard to go wrong here. Fast service, no-frills atmosphere, consistently hits. This is Camarillo comfort food at its best.

📢 What to order
Tamales · Breakfast burrito · Lengua tacos · Ceviche · Chicharrón tacos
Best for casual meals, quick lunch stops, takeout, and low-key hangouts. Not really a date-night spot — more of a "grab something really good and satisfying" place.

#2 Pick
Peruvian · Chef-Driven

Azafran

💰 $$–$$$ 🕯 Cozy & intimate ❤️ Great date night 🥂 Special occasions

Azafran is the kind of restaurant that makes you wonder why more people aren't talking about it. Peruvian cuisine done right — chef-driven, thoughtfully composed, with bold flavors you genuinely don't find anywhere else locally. It's a small, cozy spot, and that intimacy is part of what makes it special.

The Peruvian ceviche alone is worth the trip, but the pork belly chaufa and shrimp tacos keep coming up as standouts too. Everything feels considered rather than casual — this isn't a quick bite, it's a meal you actually sit with and enjoy. A true hidden gem in the best sense of the phrase.

📢 What to order
Peruvian ceviche · Pork belly chaufa · Shrimp tacos
Best for date nights, slow Sunday brunches, and dinners with friends where you want to actually enjoy the experience. Make a reservation.

#3 Pick
Filipino · Family-Run

Lola Betty's Kitchen

💰 $–$$ 🏡 Home-style 🥡 Great for takeout 🌿 Outdoor seating

There aren't many places in Camarillo doing authentic Filipino food, which makes Lola Betty's a genuine local treasure. It has that family-run, cooked-with-care feel that you just can't fake — and the portions are generous, the flavors are bold and comforting, and everything is made to order. Expect a bit of a wait, but it's worth it.

The chicken adobo is the kind of dish that makes you understand why Filipino cooking has such a loyal following. The BBQ chicken and short ribs are just as good, and the lumpia is non-negotiable as a side. Grab your order and take it outside — there are picnic tables, or you can head across the street with a drink and make an afternoon of it.

📢 What to order
Chicken adobo · BBQ chicken · Short ribs · Pancit · Lumpia (get the side, always)
Best for takeout, casual meals, and outdoor hangs. The kind of place you bring people to introduce them to Filipino food for the first time.

#4 Pick
Gastropub · American

Twisted Oak Tavern

💰 $$ 🍺 Great drinks 🎵 Live music 🌙 Open late

Twisted Oak is Camarillo's best answer to the question "where do we go for a fun night out?" It's lively, social, and the kind of place where the menu is actually big enough that everyone in your group finds something they love. The food genuinely delivers — which isn't always a given at a spot this focused on atmosphere.

The short rib nachos are legendary, the lobster mac & cheese is indulgent in exactly the right way, and the PB&J burger is one of those things that sounds weird until you try it. Strong drinks, live music most nights, and one of the few spots in town where you can keep the evening going past 9pm. The service consistently gets praised too, which matters.

📢 What to order
Short rib nachos · Lobster mac & cheese · PB&J burger · Cuban sandwich
Best for happy hour, group dinners, casual date nights, and nights out when you want good food AND good drinks AND live music in one place.

#5 Pick
Tiki Bar · Hawaiian-Inspired

House of Bamboo

💰 $$–$$$ 🌴 Immersive experience 🍹 Craft cocktails 📅 Reservations recommended

There is nothing else like House of Bamboo in Ventura County. Fully immersive tiki atmosphere — we're talking special effects, simulated rainstorms, and insane attention to detail — this place is a destination, not just a restaurant. If you have out-of-town guests, this is where you take them. No question.

The cocktails are the main event — the Painkiller and Hurricane are both standouts — but the food holds up impressively well for a tiki bar. Coconut shrimp, loco moco, spam musubi — it all fits the vibe and actually tastes great. Plan ahead: this isn't a walk-in spot, and the experience is better when you commit to it fully. Make a reservation, order the drinks, and let the atmosphere do its thing.

📢 What to order
Painkiller (cocktail) · Hurricane (cocktail) · Coconut shrimp · Loco moco · Spam musubi
Best for date nights, special occasions, birthdays, and showing off Camarillo to people who don't believe something this cool exists here. Make a reservation.
