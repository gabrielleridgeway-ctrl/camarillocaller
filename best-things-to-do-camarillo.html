Best Things To Do in Camarillo (2026)

From the Saturday farmers market and a free WWII aviation museum to a historic ranch house and coastal hiking at Point Mugu. The best things to do in Camarillo, hand-picked by The Camarillo Caller.

By Gabrielle Ridgeway Updated April 2026 🗺 8 picks 📍 Camarillo, CA
These are my personal picks based on actually living in Camarillo. I only include places I'd tell a friend to visit. None of these are paid placements.

Camarillo is the kind of place that looks like a suburb on the surface but has a lot more going on if you know where to look. These are the spots locals actually go to, from a Saturday morning ritual at the farmers market to one of the most underrated aviation museums in Southern California.

#1 Pick
Shopping

Camarillo Premium Outlets

🛍️ 150+ stores 🚶 Easy to spend hours 👥 Great for groups

If you're searching for shopping in Camarillo, this is the main destination. One of the largest outlet centers in California, featuring over 150 stores including Nike, Coach, and Crocs. It's a well-laid-out outdoor mall with a good mix of brands and an easy place to spend a few hours without feeling rushed.

📢 Don't miss
Nike Factory Store · Coach Outlet · Crocs · Toys R Us
Best for a dedicated shopping day, visitors from out of town, or anyone looking for deals on name brands.

#2 Pick
Farmers Market

Old Town Saturday Farmers Market

🗓️ Every Saturday 🕗 8:00 AM to 12:00 PM 🥕 Fresh produce + local vendors

One of the most popular weekly things to do in Camarillo. The Saturday farmers market in Old Town is one of the few places where the city really feels alive and local on a weekend morning. Fresh produce, prepared food, and vendors you won't find anywhere else.

Get there early for the best selection. It wraps up by noon and the good stuff goes fast.

📢 What to look for
Fresh produce · Prepared food · Local artisan vendors · Seasonal produce
Best for Saturday mornings, families, anyone who wants to feel like a local. Bring a tote bag.
📍 Get Directions View Website
#3 Pick
Outdoors

Camarillo Grove Park

🌳 Shaded trails 🥾 Great for hiking 🐕 Dog friendly

For outdoor activities in Camarillo, this is one of the best spots. Camarillo Grove Park offers shaded hiking trails through native oak trees in a quieter setting than most nearby parks. It's a genuinely nice place to get outside without feeling like you're just walking around a neighborhood. Bonus for dog owners: the park is off-leash during the week, which makes it one of the best spots in Camarillo to let your dog run free.

📢 Good to know
Multiple trail lengths · Shaded throughout · Picnic areas · Free to visit · Off-leash dogs welcome on weekdays
Best for hikers, dog owners (especially on weekdays for off-leash), and families looking for a low-key outdoor morning.

#4 Pick
Local History

Camarillo Ranch House

🏛️ Historic landmark 🚶 Walk the grounds free 🗓️ Weekend tours available

If you're interested in local history, the Camarillo Ranch House is one of the most unique places to visit in town. You can walk the grounds for free or join a docent-led tour on Saturdays and Sundays from 11:00 AM to 2:00 PM (last tour at 1:30 PM). Tickets are just $5 per person, cash only, and kids 12 and under are free. No reservations needed. It's the kind of place that surprises you when you finally go.

📢 Good to know
Free grounds access · Sat and Sun tours 11am to 2pm · $5 per person cash only · Kids 12 and under free · No reservations needed
Best for history buffs, first-time visitors to Camarillo, and anyone who wants to understand the city's roots.

#5 Pick
Arts + Local

Old Town Camarillo + Studio Channel Islands

🎨 Working artists 🗓️ First Saturday open studios 🛍️ Local shops + restaurants

Old Town Camarillo is one of the best areas to explore on foot, with small shops, local restaurants, and a community feel you won't get at the outlets. While you're there, stop by Studio Channel Islands Art Center at 2222 Ventura Blvd.

On the first Saturday of every month, artists open their studios to the public. It's free, low-key, and one of the more genuinely interesting things happening in the city on a regular basis.

📢 Don't miss
First Saturday open studios · Friday Night Car Cruises (weekly) · Local shops along Ventura Blvd
Best for art lovers, anyone wanting to explore local businesses, and first-Saturday visitors.
📍 Get Directions Studio Channel Islands
#6 Pick
Museum

CAF SoCal WWII Aviation Museum

✈️ Restored aircraft 🗓️ Thurs–Sat 10am–4pm · Sun 12–4pm 👨‍👩‍👧 Great for families

One of the most underrated attractions in Camarillo. Located near the airport, this museum features restored WWII aircraft and active restoration projects you can actually watch in progress. It's a genuinely impressive collection that most locals have never visited. Admission is always free, though donations are encouraged to help preserve the aircraft.

📢 Good to know
Restored WWII aircraft · Live restoration projects · Knowledgeable volunteers · Always free admission · Donations welcome
Best for families, aviation enthusiasts, and history lovers. A great rainy day option too.
📍 Get Directions View Website
#7 Pick
Nightlife

Trivia Night at Institution Ale Company

🍻 Local brewery 🗓️ Tuesdays at 7:00 PM 🎤 Consistent crowd

Looking for things to do in Camarillo at night? Local breweries and restaurants host regular events and Institution Ale Company is one of the best. Trivia every Tuesday night draws a consistent local crowd and it's a genuinely fun way to meet people in the community. Good beer, good vibe.

📢 Good to know
Trivia every Tuesday 7pm · Craft beer on tap · Food available · Free to play
Best for locals looking for a regular weeknight hangout, groups of friends, and anyone new to Camarillo wanting to meet people.
📍 Get Directions View Website
Bonus Pick
Day Trip

Point Mugu State Beach

🌊 Cliffs + long sand stretches 🥾 Coastal hiking trails 🚗 Short drive from Camarillo

One of the best beaches near Camarillo and absolutely worth the short drive. Point Mugu State Beach has cliffs, long stretches of sand, and hiking trails right off the coast that make it more than a typical beach stop. A great half-day trip or sunset destination, especially if you want something less crowded than the bigger beaches further south.

📢 Good to know
Coastal hiking trails · Less crowded than Malibu · Beautiful at sunset · Parking available
Best for a half-day escape, sunset photography, or anyone who wants a beach without the crowds.
